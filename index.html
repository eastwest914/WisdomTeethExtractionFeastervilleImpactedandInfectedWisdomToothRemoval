Frequently Asked Questions

Wisdom Teeth Extraction & Removal, Answered

How do I know if I need a wisdom tooth extraction?+

Common signs include jaw pain or swelling near the back of the mouth, gum tenderness, difficulty opening your mouth fully, or a wisdom tooth that's only partially visible — often the start of a painful wisdom tooth extraction case. An X-ray during a consultation is the most reliable way to confirm whether a wisdom tooth extraction dentist should remove one or more teeth.

Is wisdom tooth removal painful?+

The extraction itself is done under local anesthesia or sedation, so you shouldn't feel pain during the procedure. Some soreness and swelling for a few days afterward is normal, and we provide aftercare guidance and pain management options to keep recovery as comfortable as possible.

What's the difference between simple and surgical wisdom tooth extraction?+

A simple wisdom tooth extraction is for teeth that have fully erupted above the gum line. A surgical wisdom tooth extraction is needed when a wisdom tooth is impacted — trapped under the gum or bone — and requires a small incision to remove it safely.

How long does recovery from wisdom teeth removal take?+

Most patients feel noticeably better within 3 to 4 days and are mostly healed within a week to ten days. Surgical or impacted wisdom tooth removal can take slightly longer, and we'll give you a recovery timeline specific to your case.

Can you see me the same day if my wisdom tooth is infected or emergency-level painful?+

Yes. Infected or severely painful wisdom tooth extraction cases are treated as priority, and we make room for emergency wisdom tooth removal appointments whenever possible. Call 267-539-9152 and let us know it's urgent when you reach out.
